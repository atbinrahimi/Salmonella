How Did They Use Money to Win the American Revolutionary War?

What did they use the money for?

During the American revolutionary war, U.S.A. spent money on a lot of things like paying soldiers, weapons, buy food, and clothes. They also used the money to build ships, move different supplies and troops, and paid for other countries to help them. With the money that they had they also paid the loans from France and Netherlands.

how did aquire the money?

The American government only had 2 options to pay for the wars happening, the first option was to print more money and cause inflation and lower the value of their currency. The other option was to take loans off other companies. they did both, they took over 2 million dollars from France in loans and they also ended up taking loans from private Dutch investors and from the Spanish government which they struggled to pay back, you also might be wondering why they didn't tax people a lot to pay for the wars but it was because at this time the government didn't have enough power to change the amount of taxes, and also the fact that the people of America probably would not be happy getting taxed an high amount at a time of war.

How did they use the money to win the war?

U.S. needed a lot of money to keep fighting, but they didn't have enough. The government wasn't strong enough to collect lots of taxes, so they had to find other ways to get money. One way was asking other countries for help. Benjamin Franklin helped by going to France and convincing the French to give money, food, and soldiers. This support helped the American army stay active. A French nobleman named Marquis de Lafayette also helped by using his own money to support the American troops. He went to America, fought in battles, and became a close friend and helper to George Washington. George Washington used the money and help to feed, train, and supply his soldiers. Even during tough times, he kept the army together. The money helped buy weapons, clothes, food, and ships, and paid for moving the troops. In the end, all this support made a big difference. The supplies and money helped the Americans fight longer and win important battles. With help from the French army and navy, they beat the British in the final battle at Yorktown. So, the money and support helped them keep going, fight better, and win the war.
